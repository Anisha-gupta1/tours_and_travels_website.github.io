darjeeling hills

Darjeeling, Tibetan word "Dorje-ling" meaning The land of thunderbolt, lies to the extreme northern West Bengal state, northeastern India. It lies about 305 miles (490 km) north of Kolkata, at an elevation of about 7,000 feet (2,100 metres) above sea level. It is one of the most famous destinations in north east tourism and is a dream destination for all tourists who wish to spend some tranquil time amidst nature. The place that literally means “abode of god” is an ideal getaway for every tourist. Enveloped in captivating natural beauty, Darjeeling has been given the tag of the “Queen of the Hills” . Darjeeling is also a noted hill resort, and the city’s economy is based largely on tourism; the peak periods for visitors are April to June and September to November. The city has major road, rail, and air connections with Kolkata.

Four divisions of the Darjeeling hills are, namely Darjeeling, Kalimpong, Mirik and Kurseong constitute the hilly areas and the siliguri subdivision is in the plains.

The main mode of transport in Dooars is roadways.Most of the places are well-connected by roads. BEST TIME TO VISIT DARJEELING
The average rainfall of Dooars is about 3500 mm. Monsoon in Dooars generally starts from the middle of may and continues till the end of september. Summer is the mild and winters are cold and foggy in Dooar.Each season has its own charm to relish in Dooars but summer season is considered the best to visit Dooars .Every year the forest area of Dooars remains close from 15th june to 15th september.
WHERE TO STAY IN DARJEELING?
Being a favored tourist place in North Bengal for nature lovers and wilderness explorer there are many options for accommodation while travelling in Dooars. Visitors will find numerous private hotels and resorts in Dooars but Government Lodges and Forest Bunglow situated within the dense forest are most preferred place to stay in Dooars.
